# Human-understanding quality gates

## 30-second gate

A reader should be able to state:

1. who or what starts the behavior;
2. what outcome is wanted;
3. the important decisions;
4. the observable success change.

## 5-minute gate

After opening the Focus note, a reader should also be able to state:

1. where the behavior enters;
2. what rules and state changes matter;
3. why it can stop;
4. what is still unknown;
5. where to open Atlas evidence.

## Progressive-detail gate

Every hidden or collapsed detail must be one of:

- linked to an Atlas note or child drawing;
- explicitly out of scope;
- explicitly unknown;
- summarized with traceable source references.

## Prohibited shortcuts

- shrinking text until everything fits;
- deleting failure behavior without a summary;
- showing code identifiers instead of business meaning;
- calling a domain relationship a runtime flow;
- marking a question complete while a relevant unknown blocks the answer;
- using a Focus explanation as change approval for a risk-triggered boundary.
