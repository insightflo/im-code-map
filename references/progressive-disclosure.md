# Progressive disclosure rules

v5 exposes two major levels:

```text
Focus → Deep Atlas
```

Focus must disclose the path to Atlas clearly. The link label should state what detail exists there, for example:

```text
Open payment timeout, compensation, state, and test evidence
```

Avoid vague labels such as “more” or “details”. The reader should not need a scavenger hunt to discover what the next level contains. Software already supplies enough involuntary scavenger hunts.

Do not introduce a third mandatory hierarchy level. Optional child notes and diagrams can exist within Atlas, but the user-facing depth choice remains Focus or Atlas.
